Fund a Cause

Identity Design, UX/UI Design

Project Summary

Charities don’t have an easy way to accept donations from people. I designed a solution that allows charities and foundations to crowdfund donations by posting projects. Fund a Cause also allows donors to select charities and projects based on causes they would like to support.

Process

I began the project by conducting competitive analysis on competitors such as JustGiving, GoFundMe and Kickstarter. This gave me direction on how to differentiate my product and brand identity.

II decided on the name Fund a Cause to give users a clear and direct understanding of the app. My color scheme was selected to set it apart from its competitors; the bright yet slightly muted colors were chosen to invoke a feeling of inspiration and optimism along with professionalism. The words in the logo connect to each other to signify donors being able to directly connect to a cause with Fund a Cause. The logo when seen in color further illustrates this concept; the gradual color change is the donation transforming into something actionable for the cause. The typeface was carefully selected to illustrate the friendly yet professional nature of the brand. The sans-serif and curvy nature of the typeface gives off a friendly vibe, yet the strict geometric structure gives it a serious touch. Additionally, the large x-height makes it easy to read in all sizes.

After designing the identity, I created user personas to help understand the target audience. Using user stories, I then designed the user flow to efficiently achieve user goals such as signing up, creating/updating a profile, searching for projects, donating and creating/updating a project.

While creating the wireframes my goal was to keep the interface simple and intuitive. To reduce the barrier to entry for new users and encourage them to complete tasks, I broke the sign up and donation processes in small chunks. After a few iterations, I moved on to high-fidelity mockups. I wanted the visual design of the app to strike a balance between simplicity and inspiration. I noticed many of my competitors’ sites either looked exciting but cluttered or clean but lacked inspiration.

Reflections

Fund a Cause was my first web application design project and an immersive learning experience. The leap from designing a simple landing page to a complete web application made me realize the importance of consistency and structure. Additional user testing would be useful in pinpointing areas for improvement.
